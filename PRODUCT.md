# Product

## Register

product

## Users

BiasBuster is for journalists, researchers, media-literacy educators, policy analysts, and careful news readers comparing how multiple sources frame the same issue. They are usually in a review or synthesis workflow: collecting articles, checking differences in emphasis, and turning the comparison into a useful brief.

## Product Purpose

BiasBuster helps users move from article summaries to comparative framing analysis. Success means a user can quickly see what sources agree on, where their diagnoses diverge, which actors or causes are foregrounded, what language shapes perception, and what each article may underemphasize.

## Brand Personality

Analytical, civic, exacting. The product should feel like a serious newsroom research desk: calm, structured, evidence-aware, and careful with claims.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS dashboards, AI chatbot interfaces, flashy breaking-news sites, beige editorial pages, decorative gradients, oversized rounded cards, and vague “insight” copy that does not explain evidence or framing.

## Design Principles

- Lead with the comparative diagnosis, not raw article summaries.
- Make evidence and confidence visible without cluttering the reading flow.
- Use restrained interface patterns that support credibility and repeat use.
- Prefer dense, scannable structure over decorative panels.
- Treat omissions carefully with cautious language such as “may underemphasize.”

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast. Keep analytical text readable at normal zoom, provide clear focus states, avoid color-only meaning, support reduced motion, and ensure dense tables remain usable on smaller screens.
